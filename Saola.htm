The saola (Pseudoryx nghetinhensis), also called spindlehorn, Asian unicorn, or infrequently, Vu Quang bovid, is one of the world's rarest large mammals, a forest-dwelling bovine native to the Annamite Range in Vietnam and Laos. Saolas have since been kept in captivity multiple times, although only for short periods. A living wild saola was first photographed in 1999 by a camera trap set by WWF and the Vietnamese government's Forest Protection Department. It is classified under critically endangered.
