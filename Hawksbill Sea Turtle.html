The Hawksbill Sea Turtle is a critically endangered sea turtle belonging to the family Cheloniidae. It is the only extant species in the genus Eretmochelys. The species has a worldwide distribution with Atlantic and Indo-pacific subspecies. The hawksbill's appearance is similar to that of other marine turtles. In general, it has a flattened shape, a protective carapace, and flipper-like limbs, adapted for swimming in the open ocean. It is classified under critically endangered.
